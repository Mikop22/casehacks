# Scotia-Aligned Slide Master

## Master Defaults

- Canvas: 16:9, white background.
- Margins: 0.55in left/right, 0.42in top/bottom.
- Header logo: use `assets/scotiabank-public/scotiabank-logo-red-desktop-200px.svg`, top left, about 1.2-1.5in wide.
- Accent: Scotia red `#ec111a`.
- Text: charcoal `#333333`.
- Grid: two-column content grid when needed, otherwise one dominant visual per slide.
- Footer: 9-10pt gray, page number at bottom right.

For a more corporate/investor-material feel, use a red title band across the top of content slides and black table headers for data-heavy slides.

## Type Ramp

- Title slide headline: 42-48pt, bold.
- Section title: 36-44pt.
- Slide headline: 24-30pt.
- Body: 13-16pt.
- Chart labels: 9-11pt.
- Callout number: 38-56pt, red or charcoal.

Use Scotia fonts if available internally. Otherwise use Arial, Aptos, or Inter; keep weights simple.

## Layout Patterns

1. Recommendation slide
   - Headline at top.
   - Three evidence blocks in a row.
   - Red rule under the recommended action.

2. Problem slide
   - Left: one concise problem statement.
   - Right: data table, customer journey, or quote.
   - One red highlight only.

3. Product slide
   - Show workflow screenshot/mockup large.
   - Annotate with thin charcoal lines and red numbered markers.

4. Financial impact slide
   - Use charcoal/gray bars.
   - Red only for upside, decision threshold, or target scenario.

5. Roadmap slide
   - Horizontal phases: Pilot, Validate, Scale.
   - Red phase marker for current recommendation.

6. KPI slide
   - Row of red-bordered metric blocks.
   - Big value in charcoal or red.
   - One-line implication below each value.

## Deck Outline

1. Title: solution name, case prompt, team.
2. Executive recommendation: one decision sentence and three proof points.
3. Why now: customer/business trigger.
4. User insight: segment, pain point, evidence.
5. Solution concept: product promise and main workflow.
6. Demo architecture: data, model, app, controls.
7. Scotia fit: brand trust, channel fit, operational leverage.
8. Financial model: cost, benefit, payback.
9. Risk controls: privacy, compliance, explainability, rollout.
10. Roadmap and ask: 30/60/90 days and decision requested.

## Copy Rules

- Use active, banking-native phrasing.
- Each slide gets one sentence headline that says the point.
- Avoid buzzwords unless they are explained by a concrete mechanism.
- Put caveats in footnotes, not in the headline.
- Prefer Scotia-aligned business language: client, advisor, relationship, risk, controls, trusted financial partner, make it easy, growth.
